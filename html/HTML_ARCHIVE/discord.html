Discord Ethical Evaluation

UX + UI Analysis

Introduction

For my IAT 432: Design Ethics class we were tasked with evaluating an application of our choice. We chose the Discord desktop application, then conducted research, evaluated it in based on core tasks and lastly put together a presentation of our findings.

Research

First we looked at several applications and chose Discord as our team was familiar with it and often used it. We looked into online discussions about Discord’s issues to help direct our focus.

Then we familiarized ourselves with Job Yablonski’s ethical design principles, a recommended resource in class, and used it’s ethical framework to evaluate Discord’s desktop application.

Research Outcomes

Overall we found that Discord is a generally ethically well considered application. We did, however, encounter a few areas that could be improved. One such example was the breach of the Empowering principle. The application did not require authentication when viewing payment information and leaves the primary account owner vulnerable to others viewing their sensitive financial information.

Solution

As specified by our assignment, we evaluated the severity of the ethical principles breached and put together a presentation of our findings.

Project Outcomes

We learned a new ethical framework and resource to evaluate designs by. Additionally, we learned about a generally good example of design in Discord. Going back if I could change anything I may reduce my severity ratings on some of the ethical flaws we found as I believe they were not necessarily that bad, upon reflection.
