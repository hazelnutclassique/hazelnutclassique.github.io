Triggered

Short Film

Introduction

Triggered is a short film about a man's struggle with himself. Kevin finds himself in a tough situation where he must choose. Will he be able to cope with his burgeoning stress?

Role

I worked alongside 3 classmates for this project. The class was IAT 202 and the main subject was narrative filmmaking. We decided to each take turns taking charge of specific aspects of the film such as sound, directing and filming to maximize our individual learning. My role was shared in preparing logistics, storyboarding editing, directing and filming roughly a quarter of the scenes.

Process

  1. To start we chose an fun genre: a psychological thriller with a twist.
  2. Created a shots list with annotations
  3. Drew out the storyboard
  4. Aligned our schedules with actors and the team
  5. Booked all the equipment
  6. Familiarized ourselves with the equipment
  7. Began shooting on pre-planned days spread out over a few weeks
  8. Dumped the footage and began editing
  9. Recorded audio and then started sound editing
  10. Finished the final copy and presented

Storyboard

The storyboard was created after the shots list. It was a great tool for communicating our intentions with each other and teaching staff as well as guiding our execution

Shot List

We used the shot list to stay on track and make sure every shot was accounted for accurately with details of the many variables within each shot. For example, one of our shots list held the details of the actor, the props to be used, the framing of the scene, the movement of the camera and a description to help visualize.

Project Outcomes

My team was great and everyone put in a lot of effort and, I can personally say, learned a lot. As with any team, we ran into difficulties such as differences of opinion on composition and style.


We would settle our opinions by comparing, contrasting and voting on edits, shots and sounds. One big hurdle we went through was scheduling. Working in a team of 4 crew members and 4 actors involved a lot of planning. The weather was unreliable causing delays on days that would otherwise be perfectly set for filming. Nevertheless, we held our heads high and produced.
